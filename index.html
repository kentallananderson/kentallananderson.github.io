Breif Bio

I was born in Richland Washington November 1994; however, I spent most of my childhood in Portland Oregon. When I was around seven years old, I saved up for a drum set and began learning different beats and structures. A few years later I found my mother’s old classical guitar and began learning basic guitar and writing music. I started playing these songs with kids on my block through middle school in my first band. In freshman year of high school, I was the bassist and co-songwriter in my friends’ band “Life After You”, however I moved schools and the grungy alt band dissolved. After this, in the same year, me and my friend Steven Reid set out to make a nine song LP with a Shure SM 58, a Yamaha guitar, and Audacity. My mom took some photos of me and helped me learn how to use publisher to create CD sleeves, CD art, and cases. Being handmade, only about 50 were ever made. An older friend I met at summer camp earlier that year was the front man of well-liked local band called “Real Age”. He helped me book a show at The Hawthorne Theater, which was a venue that had acts like All American Rejects on a week before my set. After announcing the show, my CD managed to reach a friend of a friend, Jesse Curran. He reached out to me asking if I needed a drummer for the show. He joined the bill and we started a project that would later be known as The In-Between. We played various shows in the Portland area for a few years with Nic Kozushko being an intermediate member. We found a rhythm in using our music as a vehicle to promote various non-profit outreach, usually with benefit concerts. The peak of this project was being one of the openers at an annual tour in our city at The Roseland Theatre and standing on the same stage The Beatles once stood.

I moved to Georgia for my last year of Highschool and worked in landscaping for a year and a half. I moved to Huntsville, TX where my family now was and worked as a Barista until I was able to buy a car and move back to Portland. When I was 19, moved to Aroura Oregon. The band came back together, and we started writing for our first studio EP. During this time, we were employed to be a worship band for a church in the city. Nic, who had been working to become a sound engineer produced the first single for our EP. We had plans to continue the project, however, my mom who had been fighting cancer for the last 6 years was in her final weeks. I flew back to TX and ended up staying after she passed. I was now 21 and working as a server at a local restaurant. I ended up becoming a barista again after an employee re-recommended me for the job. She was a master’s student working toward her degree in counselling at the local university. We got married about 2 years later.

During this time, I had recorded and released the EP me and Jesse started with my friend Joel Armenderiz in a project called Taylor Court. Taylor Court opened for Sheldon Cole on his indie North American tour.

I had the opportunity to meet a great number of talented wonderful people on the road and around TX playing shows for a couple years.

The Local Hero EP lived up to its name by creating a local buzz that fizzled out. Unfortunately, the project didn’t gain the traction it needed to continue. We released one more single a year later and wrote another EP we began to demo, but Joel moved to Austin with his now wonderful wife Susie. He is now a developer at IBM. The sound evolved to something much more engaging and musically intelligent; unfortunately, time was not on its side. This project dissolved as well.

Though some expectations weren’t met, I never regret my choice of pursuing music. It was an experience that can't be quantified monetarily.

I worked odd jobs and began working on my bachelor’s degree in business management at a local community college. After a year of school, my wife became pregnant with our baby girl to come and it became very clear odd jobs and student loans for three more years wouldn’t suffice. I took two semesters off school, began to learn Python, and started courses at Actualize Chicago to develop a skill valued in the market. I ended up becoming fascinated with this field only two weeks into the program. I still write music and lyrics for the project Allantown. One EP is written and has begun its demoing process; however, it could be five years until it is released, as this is now only a hobby of mine.

Whats next for me, you may ask?
